If you own a Beagle, you know the "nose-down, ears-off" phenomenon. Once a Beagle catches a scent, their recall often vanishes, replaced by a single-minded drive to follow the trail. This biological trait makes Beagles one of the most common breeds to get lost.

In this 2026 guide, we review the best GPS tracking technology specifically tested for scent-driven hounds.

### 3. Hardware: The "Escape-Proof" Collar
A GPS unit is useless if the Beagle slips the collar.

*   **Martingale Collars**: We recommend mounting your GPS unit on a Martingale-style collar. These tighten slightly when the dog pulls back, preventing the Beagle from "backing out" of their gear—a common trick for this breed.

---

### Beagle Tracker Strategy
1. **Set Geofences**: Create a 200ft "Home" zone.
2. **Battery Management**: Check your tracker's battery every Sunday. Most cellular models last 2-4 weeks; satellite models last 24-48 hours of active use.
3. **ID Backup**: Even with a $500 GPS, always have a physical ID tag with your phone number engraved.
