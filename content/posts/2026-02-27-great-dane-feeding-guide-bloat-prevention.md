### 1. The Elevated Feeder Debate: High or Low?
For decades, elevated feeders were standard for Great Danes to "improve digestion." However, recent veterinary studies (including those from Purdue University) suggest that **elevation might actually increase the risk of bloat** in some giant breeds by encouraging faster air intake.

*   **Our Recommendation**: Consult your vet. Many modern Dane owners are moving back to **floor-level feeding** combined with **slow-feeders** to minimize air gulping.
*   **The Mobility Exception**: If your Great Dane has advanced arthritis or "Wobbler Syndrome," an elevated feeder like the **Pet Zone Designer Diner** is necessary to reduce neck and joint strain.

### 3. Life-Saving Infrastructure: Gastropexy
While gear helps, it is not a cure. We strongly recommend discussing a **Prophylactic Gastropexy** (stomach tacking) with your veterinarian. This surgery doesn't prevent "bloat" (gas), but it prevents the "twist" (volvulus), which is the fatal part of the condition.

---

### Great Dane Safety Feeding Rules
1. **Rest Period**: No vigorous exercise 1 hour before or 2 hours after meals.
2. **Multiple Small Meals**: Feed 3 times a day instead of one large "gorge" meal.
3. **Hydration Control**: Do not allow your Dane to gulp massive amounts of water immediately after eating dry kibble.
